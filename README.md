# ST-STM32WL3R-Workshop
 
Throughout the **STM32WL3R workshop**, you explored the **STM32WL3R**, ST’s wireless SoC designed for **ultra-low-power remote-control** and **sub-GHz IoT** applications.
 
You discovered how the STM32WL3R combines an **Arm® Cortex®-M0+ core**, an **integrated sub-GHz radio**, a **wideband wake-up receiver**, and **multiple modulation support via IQ interface** to deliver flexible and energy-efficient wireless designs. You also learned about its **six wake-up pins**, **ultra-deep stop modes**, **up to 18 GPIOs**, and support for **169, 315, 433, 868, and 920 MHz bands**.
 
During the session, you followed guided hands-on activities covering **RF transmission testing**, **pre-certification measurements**, **current consumption in ultra-deep stop**, and a **remote-control receiver use case in OOK direct mode** using ST firmware, tools, and the **NUCLEO-WL3RKBx** board.
 
You will find in this repository all the material used during the workshop and the associated collateral to replicate the activities on your own.

## How to replicate
 
To replicate the workshop activities, you will need:
 
- **NUCLEO-WL3RKBx** (Available on st.com)
- **STM32CubeIDE**
- **STM32CubeProgrammer**
- **STM32CubeWL3** firmware package and associated examples
- **STM32CubeWise-RadioExplorer** tool for RF evaluation (download [here](https://www.st.com/en/development-tools/STM32CubeWISEre.html))
- Test equipment for **RF** and **current consumption** measurements ([STLINK-V3PWR](https://www.st.com/en/development-tools/stlink-v3pwr.html) tool)
